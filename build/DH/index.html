Intro to Asymmetric Cryptography

DIffie-hellman key exchange

Public Key Cryptography is a form of asymmetric cryptography, where there is a private and public keys...
One example is the Diffie-Hellman key exchange protocol.

To understand how Diffie-Hellman works, lets take a look at the math behind it.

Source: Wikipedia

Notice that what makes this protocol works is the properties of exponentiation. Namely that, g to the b to the power of a is the same as g to the a to the power of b.

From this visual representation, we can see that it is not possible to get back the original secret integers assuming that mixture separation is expensive. Mathematically, that would be when our value of p is big enough to make it difficult to calculate the discrete log x of g^x.
